# Clause & Effect — cold DM & outreach templates

Rule of thumb: lead with **value, not a pitch**. You're offering a free favour, not asking
for a sale. Personalise the first line every time (mention their actual content). Send 10–20 a day.

---

## 1. Micro-creator DM (Instagram / TikTok) — the free-review offer
> Hey [name] — love your [specific content, e.g. "skincare hauls"] 🙌
> Quick one: I'm a London law student and I run a service that reads brand-deal contracts in plain English and flags the clauses that could cost creators money (exclusivity traps, usage rights, etc).
> I'm doing free reviews for a few creators this month. Want me to check your next brand deal — no charge, no catch?

## 2. Follow-up (if no reply after 3–4 days)
> No worries if you're busy! Offer still stands — if a brand deal lands and you want a second pair of eyes before signing, just send it over. Free 👍

## 3. After you've reviewed one (turn it into a testimonial)
> Glad that was useful! 🙌 Tiny favour — would you mind if I shared a screenshot of the breakdown (your details blurred) as a testimonial? And if you ever know a creator who'd want the same, send them my way 🙏

## 4. Freelancer / designer (LinkedIn or email)
> Hi [name] — saw you do [freelance design/dev] work. I run a plain-English contract-check service for freelancers (client agreements, IP, payment terms). I'm offering free first reviews this month to build the business. If you've got a contract coming up, happy to flag anything risky before you sign — want me to take a look?

## 5. Reddit / Discord / Facebook group reply (NOT a DM — public, helpful)
> This clause is worth pushing back on — [explain the specific issue in 2 lines]. Exclusivity that long with no extra pay isn't standard; you can ask to cap it at 30 days. (I actually built a free tool that reads contracts in plain English and flags this stuff — happy to run yours through it if useful, no charge.)

## 6. Local / uni angle (your Students' Union, course group chats)
> Hey everyone — I built a free tool that reads contracts in plain English (tenancy agreements, freelance gigs, internships) and tells you what to watch for. First check's free if anyone's signing something and wants a second opinion 👀 [link]

## 7. Warm intro ask (to friends with audiences)
> Hey [name] — launching a little contract-review business and looking for my first few users. Know anyone who freelances, creates content, or is signing a tenancy soon? First review's free — would love an intro 🙏

---

### Outreach rules
- **Personalise line 1** or it reads as spam and gets you nowhere.
- **One ask per message.** Don't pitch the paid tiers in a cold DM — just the free review.
- **Track it:** a simple note of who you messaged + replies. Aim 10–20/day for 2 weeks.
- **Never imply you're a qualified solicitor.** You explain contracts in plain English and flag risks — say exactly that.
